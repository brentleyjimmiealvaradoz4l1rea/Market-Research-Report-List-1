This growth can be attributed to the increasing prevalence of gluten-related disorders and the growing consumer awareness about gluten-free diets. Consumers are becoming more health-conscious and are seeking out alternative flour options that can cater to their dietary needs.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the low-gluten flour market is the rising demand for organic and non-GMO products. Manufacturers are responding to this trend by offering low-gluten flour that is certified organic and non-GMO. Additionally, the market is seeing the introduction of new innovative blends of low-gluten flour that combine various gluten-free grains for improved texture and flavor in baked goods.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Low-gluten Flour Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Machine Milled Flour</li><li>Stone Ground Flour</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Machine milled flour is produced using high-speed steel rollers which grind the grains into fine powder. This process results in a consistent texture and is more efficient in terms of production. On the other hand, stone ground flour is produced by grinding grains between two large stone wheels, resulting in a coarser texture and preserving more of the natural nutrients and flavors of the grains. Both types of flour have their own unique characteristics and are popular choices in the low-gluten flour market.</p></p>
