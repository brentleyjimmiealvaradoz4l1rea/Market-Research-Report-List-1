<p><strong>The GPIO Expanders Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Quasi-bidirectional GPIO</li><li>LED PWM Open-drain GPIO</li></ul></p>
<p><p>GPIO expanders are devices that increase the number of available GPIO pins on a microcontroller. Quasi-bidirectional GPIO expanders allow pins to be configured as either inputs or outputs, providing flexibility in managing signals. LED PWM expanders enable pulse-width modulation control for varying the brightness of LEDs. Open-drain GPIO expanders are able to sink current when driving outputs low, making them suitable for interfacing with devices that use open-drain signaling. Each type of expander serves different purposes in expanding the capabilities of microcontrollers and improving system functionality.</p></p>
