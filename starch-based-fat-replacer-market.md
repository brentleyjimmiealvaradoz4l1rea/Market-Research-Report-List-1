<p><ul><li>Microparticulated Starch</li><li>Modified Starch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Starch-based fat replacers are commonly categorized into two main types: microparticulated starch and modified starch. Microparticulated starch is produced by breaking down starch molecules into tiny particles, which mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats in food products. On the other hand, modified starch undergoes chemical or physical alterations to improve its functionality as a fat replacer. Both types of starch-based fat replacers are widely used in the food industry to reduce the fat content in various food products while maintaining their taste and texture.</p></p>
