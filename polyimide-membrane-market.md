<p><ul><li>Both Benzenoid</li><li>Biphenyl Polyimide Membrane</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyimide membranes are divided into two main types based on their chemical structure: Benzenoid and Biphenyl Polyimide Membranes. Benzenoid Polyimide Membranes are composed of a repeating benzene ring structure, providing high thermal stability and excellent mechanical properties. On the other hand, Biphenyl Polyimide Membranes contain biphenyl groups in their structure, offering improved chemical resistance and solubility properties. Both types of membranes are widely used in various applications such as gas separation, water treatment, and electronic devices due to their unique combination of properties.</p></p>
