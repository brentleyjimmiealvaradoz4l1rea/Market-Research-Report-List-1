<p><ul><li>Single Color</li><li>Dual Color</li><li>Multi-color</li></ul></p>
<p><p>FISH (Fluorescence in situ hybridization) testing probes are used in genetic analysis to detect specific DNA sequences. The market for these probes can be classified into three types based on the number of colors used for labeling the probes: Single Color, Dual Color, and Multi-color. Single color probes use one fluorescent label to detect a target DNA sequence, while dual color probes use two different labels for two different sequences. Multi-color probes use three or more labels to detect multiple target sequences simultaneously, allowing for more complex genetic analysis.</p></p>
