<p><p>Molecular diagnosis of rectal cancer involves various techniques such as PCR (polymerase chain reaction), FISH (fluorescent in situ hybridization), DNA sequencing, and gene chip analysis. PCR is used to amplify DNA segments for detection of mutations. FISH is used to visualize genetic changes. DNA sequencing helps in identifying specific genetic mutations. Gene chip analysis involves studying the expression of thousands of genes simultaneously. These methods play a crucial role in accurate and early diagnosis of rectal cancer, aiding in personalized treatment strategies.</p></p>
