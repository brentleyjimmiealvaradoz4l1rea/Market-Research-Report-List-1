<p><p>Digital Mapping Cameras (DMC) come in various types based on their bit depth for capturing images. These types include 8-bit DMC, 10-bit DMC, 12-bit DMC, 14-bit DMC, 16-bit DMC, and others. The bit depth refers to the number of bits used to represent colors in each pixel of the image. A higher bit depth allows for more accurate color representation and smoother gradients in the images. Different DMC types cater to various requirements and preferences in the digital mapping industry.</p></p>
