<p><ul><li>Monocanalicular Stents</li><li>Bicanalicular Stents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nasolacrimal stents are used to treat tear duct obstructions. Monocanalicular stents are inserted into one tear duct, while bicanalicular stents are inserted into both tear ducts. Monocanalicular stents are often preferred in cases where only one duct is blocked, providing a more targeted approach. Bicanalicular stents are used when both ducts are blocked or to provide additional support. Both types of stents help to maintain the patency of the tear ducts and promote proper tear drainage.</p></p>
