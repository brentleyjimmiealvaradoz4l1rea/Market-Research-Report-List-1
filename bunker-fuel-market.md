<p><p>Bunker fuel, also known as marine fuel, is a type of fuel oil used on ships. It is a heavy fuel oil that is derived from crude oil and is used to power the engines of large vessels. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for marine transportation, especially in emerging economies. The rising international trade and shipping activities are driving the demand for bunker fuel. Additionally, the implementation of regulations such as IMO 2020, which mandates the use of low-sulfur fuels in ships, is also expected to boost the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the bunker fuel market is the increasing adoption of alternative fuels such as LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas) and biofuels. These fuels are cleaner and more environmentally friendly compared to traditional bunker fuels, and many shipping companies are investing in technologies to switch to these alternative fuels. This shift towards cleaner fuels is expected to drive innovation and growth in the bunker fuel market in the coming years.</p></p>
