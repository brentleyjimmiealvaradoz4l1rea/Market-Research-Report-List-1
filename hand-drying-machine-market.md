<p><p>Hand drying machines can be categorized into two main types: hand-in and hand-under. Hand-in machines require users to insert their hands into the machine for drying, while hand-under machines have a nozzle where users place their hands underneath to be dried. </p><p>Hand-in machines are typically more efficient in drying hands quickly but may not be as hygienic as hand-under machines, which reduce the risk of cross-contamination. Both types have their own advantages and are commonly found in public restrooms and commercial settings.</p></p>
