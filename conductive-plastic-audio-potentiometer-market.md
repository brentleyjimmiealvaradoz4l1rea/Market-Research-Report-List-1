<p><ul><li>Wire-Wound</li><li>Non-wire Wound</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Conductive plastic audio potentiometers are available in two main types: wire-wound and non-wire wound. Wire-wound potentiometers use a resistive wire wound around a core, providing precise control over audio levels. Non-wire wound potentiometers use conductive plastic material to achieve the same effect without the need for wire winding. Both types offer smooth and accurate control over audio levels, with wire-wound potentiometers generally providing higher precision but non-wire wound potentiometers offering a more cost-effective solution.</p></p>
